# Situation
A reversible decision is treated as final, so over-analysis substitutes for action and anxiety grows.

# Approaches Tried
Setting a hard deadline; writing explicit decision criteria; naming the concrete fears; talking it through once with a trusted person.

# What Worked
A fixed 48-hour deadline plus written criteria converts dread into a decision. Naming the 3 specific fears shrinks them.

# What Didn't
Open-ended "more research" and waiting to "feel certain" deepened the avoidance.

# Lesson / Principle
Most decisions are reversible and the cost of delay is real; past a point, speed beats certainty.
