# Key Ideas
- Sleep is the single most effective thing you can do to reset brain and body each day. It is an active, structured process — not passive shutdown.
- Two main types: **non-REM** (stages 1–4; stages 3–4 = deep sleep) and **REM** (rapid eye movement / dream sleep). They cycle every ~90 minutes.
- **First half of the night is dominated by deep non-REM; second half is dominated by REM.** Cutting sleep short selectively starves you of REM.
- Quality matters as much as quantity. Sleep efficiency (time asleep / time in bed) should be ≥ 85%. Brief awakenings are normal — only worry if you can't get back to sleep within ~25 min, or if it happens many times per night.
- Two regulators of sleep: **circadian rhythm** (the ~24h internal clock, anchored by light, temperature, food, activity) and **adenosine pressure** (a chemical that builds up the longer you're awake and pushes you toward sleep).
- Caffeine works by **blocking the adenosine receptor**, not removing adenosine. Half-life ~5–6h, quarter-life ~10–12h. When it clears, the accumulated adenosine hits you = "caffeine crash."
- Alcohol is a **sedative, not a sleep aid.** It fragments sleep, blocks REM, and drops growth hormone release ≥50%. Even one glass with dinner measurably reduces REM.
- THC speeds sleep onset but blocks REM and causes withdrawal rebound insomnia. CBD data is thin; possibly beneficial via temperature drop, anxiolytic effect, or adenosine signaling — but supplement purity is highly variable.
- Melatonin is a timing signal, not a sleep generator. Meta-analysis: only ~3.9 extra minutes of sleep, ~2.2% efficiency gain. Typical supplement doses (5–10 mg) are 10–20× physiological. Useful mainly in older adults (calcified pineal).
- REM sleep is the strongest sleep-stage predictor of longevity (linear relationship in large-population machine-learning analysis): less REM → higher all-cause mortality.
- Sleep, sex, and reproductive hormones are deeply coupled: sleep loss → lower testosterone/estrogen, disrupted menstrual cycles; sex with orgasm and pair-bonding hormones (oxytocin, prolactin) promote sleep onset. One extra hour of sleep ↑ women's sexual interest by ~14%.
- Sleep loss erodes empathy, increases relationship conflict, and lowers conflict-resolution success.

# Practical Lessons
**Light & circadian anchor**
- Get **30–40 min of natural daylight in the eyes early in the day** (a cloudy day outside is still vastly brighter than indoor lighting). Exercise near an east-facing window stacks two circadian cues at once.
- Hold a **fixed wake time** every day — it is the strongest single anchor of the circadian rhythm.

**Caffeine**
- Stop caffeine **8–10 hours before your typical bedtime**. Late caffeine can drop deep sleep by up to 30% even when you "fall asleep fine" — equivalent to aging your sleep by 10–12 years.

**Alcohol**
- There is no "safe" dose for sleep quality — even one glass measurably reduces REM and growth-hormone release. If you drink, earlier in the day is less bad than near bedtime.

**Naps**
- If you sleep fine at night, naps are great (NASA: 26-min nap → +34% performance, +50% alertness). Keep them **20–25 min** to avoid grogginess; cut off naps **6–8h before bedtime**.
- **If you have insomnia, do not nap** — it bleeds off the adenosine pressure you need for night sleep.

**After a bad night, do NOTHING differently** (Michael Perlis rule):
- Don't sleep in. Don't nap. Don't add caffeine. Don't go to bed earlier.
- Any of these reduces the next night's adenosine pressure and prolongs the insomnia.

**Wind-down routine** (sleep is a landing plane, not a light switch)
- 60+ min of dim light, screens off, light stretching / meditation / reading / warm bath.
- Bedroom cool (~18–19°C / 65–67°F). Core body temp must drop ~1°C to fall and stay asleep — this is why temperature is the master lever.

**Worry journal** (1–2 hours before bed, not in bed)
- Write down every concern and to-do. Studies show this **cuts sleep-onset time by ~50%** — on par with pharmaceuticals. Closes the "open browser tabs" of the mind.

**Remove all clock faces from the bedroom** (including the phone). Knowing it's 3:22 AM only makes a bad night worse.

**Don't count sheep** — it makes it harder to fall asleep. Instead, take a detailed **mental walk** through a familiar place.

**Supplements — what the data actually says**
- **Melatonin:** weak effect in healthy adults; physiological dose is 0.1–0.3 mg, not 5–10 mg. Useful mainly in 60+.
- **Magnesium:** no good evidence in non-deficient healthy adults. Threonate (crosses blood-brain barrier) is the most interesting candidate but understudied.
- **Valerian:** 5 of 7 good studies show no benefit.
- **Tart cherry juice:** 3 RCTs — added 34–84 min of sleep, reduced awake time by >1h. Preliminary but promising.
- **Kiwi fruit** (with skin): one human + animal study — faster sleep onset, longer sleep; mechanism appears to involve GABA system.
- **Apigenin (chamomile derivative):** subjective benefits, no hard objective data.
- **Tryptophan / serotonin:** highly variable — can cause vivid dreams and multi-day insomnia rebound; risks fragmenting REM.

**Order of intervention for any sleep problem:** behavioral → nutrition → supplementation → prescription → devices. Most sleep gains are at the behavioral layer.

**CBT-I (cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia)** is at least as effective as sleeping pills, more durable (benefits last ~a decade in some studies), and has no rebound.

# Philosophical Lessons
- Fighting biology is a losing game; the loss shows up as disease, sickness, impairment. Sleep is non-negotiable.
- "I'll sleep when I'm dead" is exactly backwards — poor sleep brings death sooner.
- Sleep is a civil right, not a luxury or a sign of laziness. Society has stigmatized rest; the stigma is wrong.
- Discipline around sleep (fixed wake time, no late caffeine, wind-down) is not punishment — it is freedom from chronic impairment.
- Public-health messaging works best with both gas and brake: be faithful to the science but don't terrorize people who are already struggling.

# Emotional Lessons
- Sleep loss raises emotional sensitivity — "a finer grain of sandpaper" — small triggers become big reactions; empathy drops; relationship conflict rises and resolves worse.
- REM sleep is **overnight therapy / emotional first aid** — it metabolizes the day's emotional charge. Block REM (alcohol, THC, very late nights) and you wake emotionally raw.
- Anxiety at 3–4 AM is qualitatively different from daytime anxiety — disproportionately catastrophizing. Learn not to trust those thoughts; get them on paper before bed.
- A wired-and-tired state (deeply tired but cannot fall asleep) is a sympathetic-nervous-system / cortisol problem, not a sleep-drive problem. Address it by lowering arousal, not by lying in bed earlier.

# Possible Use Cases
- **Irregular sleep / daily headaches** (PRB-20260607-02): anchor wake time, screens off 60 min pre-bed, cut caffeine 8–10h before bed, cool bedroom, worry journal.
- **Insomnia or middle-of-the-night waking:** "do nothing" rule + CBT-I + worry journal + remove clock faces. Don't compensate the next day.
- **Low energy / afternoon slump:** consider a 20-min nap (only if night sleep is solid); morning sunlight; no afternoon caffeine.
- **Emotional reactivity, easy frustration, relationship friction:** check sleep first — REM debt is a likely upstream cause.
- **Decision-making, learning, memory, creativity:** protect the second half of the night (REM-rich).
- **Hormonal concerns (testosterone, estrogen, libido, menstrual regularity):** sleep quantity AND quality are upstream of all of these.
- **Travel / red-eye flights:** known to cause days of malaise. Avoid where possible; if unavoidable, anchor wake time aggressively on arrival.

# Summary
The non-negotiables of good sleep are: a **fixed wake time**, **morning sunlight in the eyes**, **no caffeine in the 8–10h before bed**, **little to no alcohol**, a **cool bedroom**, a **60-minute wind-down with screens off**, and a **worry journal** an hour or two before bed. Quality is at least as important as duration; aim for ~7–9 hours with high sleep efficiency. Supplements are mostly weak or unstudied; tart cherry and kiwi are interesting, melatonin is overhyped (and overdosed), valerian doesn't work. Don't nap if you have insomnia. After a bad night, do nothing differently — let pressure rebuild. REM sleep (second half of the night) is the strongest sleep-stage predictor of longevity and the emotional regulator of the brain; alcohol and THC silently destroy it. Sleep is upstream of mood, hormones, learning, relationships, and lifespan — work the behavioral levers first, then everything else.
